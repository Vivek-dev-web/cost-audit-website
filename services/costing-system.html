Costing System Installation

Custom design and implementation of product-wise costing systems — from ABC Costing to Standard Costing and ERP integration advisory.

Why It Matters

A Robust Costing System — The Foundation of Compliance & Profitability

Without a well-designed product costing system, companies cannot accurately determine the cost of production needed for statutory cost audit, GST on captive consumption (CAS-4), transfer pricing, or management decision-making.

A good costing system also reveals true product profitability, enables meaningful variance analysis, and forms the basis for pricing decisions, capacity utilisation monitoring, and cost reduction initiatives.

Our team of CMAs with engineering backgrounds designs costing frameworks that are both CAS-compliant and operationally practical for your production environment.

Activity-Based Costing (ABC)

Allocates costs based on activities consumed — ideal for multi-product, service-heavy environments.

Standard Costing

Predetermined cost benchmarks for materials, labour, and overheads with variance analysis.

Job / Batch Costing

For custom manufacturers and job shops — cost tracked by order/batch/project.

Process Costing

For continuous manufacturing processes like cement, sugar, chemicals — cost tracked by process stage.

Implementation Steps

Our Costing System Implementation Process

1. Current State Assessment

Review of existing cost records, production process flow, cost centres, and ERP/accounting software configuration.

2. System Design

Design of cost centre structure, cost driver identification, overhead allocation bases, and product costing template.

3. Implementation & Integration

Configuration of cost accounting module in your ERP (SAP CO, Oracle, Tally, or others), data migration, and testing.

4. Team Training

Hands-on training for finance, production, and MIS teams on data collection, cost recording, and report generation.

5. Parallel Run

Parallel running of new and old systems for 1–3 months to validate accuracy, identify gaps, and refine allocations.

6. Go-Live & Support

Go-live support, monthly review of cost statements for 6 months, and annual system health check.
